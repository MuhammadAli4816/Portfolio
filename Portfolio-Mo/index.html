Self Navigating Vacuum

WHAT: Vacuum that navigates throughout the floor and avoids walls or obstacles using 3 IR Infrared Obstacle Avoidance Sensors.

HOW: 3 infrared sensors are placed to watch the left, right, and front of the robot. Once the sensor detects an object the robot will turn a specified amount depending on which sensor was detected for how long. Continuous servo motors and sensors are placed on a 3D printed platform. All motors and sensors were programmed in C++ and connected to an Arduino Uno.

RESULTS: Successfully navigates through mazes, corners, or walls while simultaneously sucking in debris without any external assistance.

Medicine Sorter

WHAT: Three-part mechanism utilizing a 3D printed funnel, outer shell, and inner vessel that separates pills by their unique shapes and dimensions.

HOW: Pills are initially put through the funnel, and will fall into a slot based on their size. Once the user twists the inner vessel counter-clockwise each pill will then fall out of its corresponding hole. Each part was designed on Fusion360 and built using a 3D printer.

RESULTS: Separates zinc sulfate and advil pills with 99% accuracy(only source of error being human error).

Ball Launcher

WHAT: Replica of the product "Ball Launcher" used by sports teams such as Manchester United, Chelsea, and Arsenal.

HOW: Ball is shot into the air by horizontal force provided by two continous servos. To give the user more control on where the ball will land, a thrid motor is connected to the base to allow for a person to aim left/right.

RESULTS: Maximum distance a ball was shot was 5 inches.
